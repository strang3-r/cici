1. **Dual Polity:** Existence of central and state governments, each with its own sphere of authority.
2. **Written Constitution:** Clearly defines the powers and functions of both the Union and State governments.
3. **Division of Powers:** Powers are divided between the Union and State governments through the Union List, State List, and Concurrent List.
4. **Supremacy of the Constitution:** The Constitution is the supreme law, and both Union and State governments must operate within its framework.
5. **Rigid Constitution:** Amendments to the Constitution, especially those affecting the federal structure, require the consent of both the Union and the States.
6. **Independent Judiciary:** An independent judiciary acts as an umpire, resolving disputes between the Union and the States and interpreting the Constitution.
7. **Bicameral Legislature:** The Union Parliament has two houses (Lok Sabha and Rajya Sabha). The Rajya Sabha represents the states and its composition is based on the population of states, ensuring states have a voice at the national level.
