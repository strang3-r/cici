## Introduction

- Fundamental Duties are the obligations of citizens towards the nation.
- They are a reminder that rights and responsibilities go hand in hand.

## Origin of Fundamental Duties

- **Initial Omission:** The original Constitution did not contain any provision for Fundamental Duties.
- **Swaran Singh Committee (1976):** Recommended the inclusion of a separate chapter on Fundamental Duties.
- **42nd Constitutional Amendment Act, 1976:** Inserted **Part IVA** into the Constitution, titled "Fundamental Duties".
- **Article 51A:** This article enlists the Fundamental Duties.

## Key Points for UPSC Aspirants

- **Non-Justiciable:** Fundamental Duties are not enforceable by any court of law. They are moral obligations. âš–ï¸
- **Complementary to Fundamental Rights:** They are seen as a counterpart to the Fundamental Rights.
- **Importance:** Though non-enforceable, they are crucial for the development of a responsible citizenry and for the overall progress of the nation.
- **Amendments:**
    - 42nd Amendment Act, 1976: Introduced 10 duties.
    - 86th Amendment Act, 2002: Added the 11th duty related to education.
- **UPSC Relevance:** These duties are important for essays, ethics papers (GS-IV), and general polity questions (GS-II).
