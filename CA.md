| Amendment Number | Objective                                                                                                        |
| :--------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1st Amendment    | To abolish Zamindari system, etc. To give effect to certain policy decisions and the new articles 31A and 31B. |
| 2nd Amendment    | Readjustment of representation in the Lok Sabha by territorial constituencies.                                 |
| 4th Amendment    | Various provisions relating to acquisition of property, fundamental rights.                                      |
| 7th Amendment    | Reorganization of states on linguistic basis.                                                                    |
| 9th Amendment    | To make certain provisions of the Indian Penal Code, Criminal Procedure Code, and Indian Evidence Act applicable to Jammu and Kashmir. |
| 10th Amendment   | Incorporation of Dadra and Nagar Haveli as a Union Territory.                                                    |
| 12th Amendment   | Incorporation of Goa, Daman and Diu as a Union Territory.                                                        |
| 13th Amendment   | Formation of Nagaland as a state.                                                                                |
| 15th Amendment   | Increased the retirement age of High Court judges from 60 to 62.                                                 |
| 16th Amendment   | To give the states freedom to legislate on the subjects of education and currency.                               |
| 19th Amendment   | Abolished the system of election tribunals.                                                                      |
| 21st Amendment   | Included Sindhi as one of the official languages in the Eighth Schedule.                                         |
| 24th Amendment   | Asserted the Parliament's power to amend any part of the Constitution, including Fundamental Rights.            |
| 25th Amendment   | Restricted the right to property, and also gave primacy to some Directive Principles over Fundamental Rights.     |
| 26th Amendment   | Abolished the privy purses and privileges of the former rulers of Indian states.                                 |
| 29th Amendment   | Placed certain Acts relating to land reforms in the Ninth Schedule, thus immunizing them from judicial review. |
| 31st Amendment   | Increased the number of Lok Sabha seats from 525 to 545.                                                         |
| 35th Amendment   | Made Sikkim a "protectorate" of India.                                                                           |
| 36th Amendment   | Made Sikkim a full-fledged state of the Indian Union.                                                            |
| 42nd Amendment   | Introduced the terms "Socialist," "Secular," and "Integrity" in the Preamble. Extended the tenure of Lok Sabha and state assemblies to 6 years. |
| 44th Amendment   | Reverted the tenure of Lok Sabha and state assemblies back to 5 years. Restored the original position of Fundamental Rights. |
| 52nd Amendment   | Provided for disqualification of members of Parliament and state legislatures on the ground of defection (Anti-defection law). |
| 56th Amendment   | Formation of Goa as a state.                                                                                     |
| 61st Amendment   | Reduced the voting age from 21 years to 18 years.                                                                |
| 65th Amendment   | Provided for the establishment of a Special Officer to investigate into matters relating to the safeguards for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes. |
| 69th Amendment   | Conferred a special status on Delhi, designated it as the National Capital Territory of Delhi.                  |
| 71st Amendment   | Included Konkani, Manipuri, and Nepali in the Eighth Schedule.                                                  |
| 73rd Amendment   | Gave constitutional status to Panchayati Raj institutions, added Part IX and the Eleventh Schedule.               |
| 74th Amendment   | Gave constitutional status to Urban Local Bodies, added Part IX-A and the Twelfth Schedule.                     |
| 77th Amendment   | Provided for reservation in promotions for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes in government employment.        |
| 84th Amendment   | Entitled the government to redraft the delimitation of constituencies.                                           |
| 86th Amendment   | Made Right to Education a Fundamental Right for children between 6 and 14 years of age.                         |
| 91st Amendment   | Limited the size of the Council of Ministers at the Centre and in the States to 15% of the total membership of the House. |
| 92nd Amendment   | Included Bodo, Dogri, Maithili, and Santhali in the Eighth Schedule.                                             |
| 93rd Amendment   | Provided for reservation for Other Backward Classes (OBCs) in admission to educational institutions.             |
| 97th Amendment   | Provided for the promotion of cooperative societies as autonomous bodies.                                        |
| 99th Amendment   | Established the National Judicial Appointments Commission (NJAC). (Later struck down by the Supreme Court).     |
| 100th Amendment  | Exchange of certain enclaves between India and Bangladesh.                                                       |
| 101st Amendment  | Introduced the Goods and Services Tax (GST).                                                                     |
| 102nd Amendment  | Granted constitutional status to the National Commission for Backward Classes (NCBC).                           |
| 103rd Amendment  | Provided for 10% reservation for economically weaker sections (EWS) in government jobs and educational institutions. |
| 104th Amendment  | Extended the reservation of seats for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes in the Lok Sabha and state assemblies for another ten years. |
| 105th Amendment  | Empowered states and Union Territories to create their own lists of socially and educationally backward classes. |
